Reach is the most overrated metric in B2B marketing.

Everyone wants to get in front of more people. Bigger audience, more impressions, wider net.

But buyers don't buy because they saw you once. They buy because they saw you eight or ten times, in the right context, until you became the obvious choice. That's the part most teams miss.

Consumption drives conversion. The same person seeing your stuff repeatedly is worth more than ten new people seeing it once.

So the play isn't a bigger audience. It's a tighter one, hit more often.

Take your warmest few hundred people. The ones who already visited, engaged, or sit in your CRM. Filter them down to your ICP. Then show up consistently, across formats, until buying feels inevitable.

That's how you manufacture the "I feel like I see you everywhere" reaction on a small budget. Not by reaching everyone. By being impossible to ignore for the few hundred people who actually matter.

Most B2B companies are sitting on 3 to 5 warm audiences they've never once activated. And they're spending the whole budget chasing cold strangers instead.

Here's what we see across 80+ clients and $100M+ in managed LinkedIn spend... warm audiences convert at 3 to 5x cold, at 40 to 60% lower cost on retargeting. Yet almost everyone starts at the coldest, most expensive end of the pool. Backwards.

The fix isn't a bigger budget. It's order.

You start with the people closest to buying (your pipeline, your site visitors, your CRM, the connections who already fit your ICP) and you EARN your way out toward cold. Cold is the last tier, not the first.

And the part nobody talks about... on LinkedIn you can layer professional filters (title, company, industry, seniority) on top of ALL your retargeting audiences. No other platform lets you do that. So "I see you everywhere" actually means the RIGHT people see you everywhere... not your competitor's intern and your aunt.

That's the whole game. The audiences are already there. Most people just never built the architecture to use them.
