9 months into building my first business, Refine Labs, nobody took me seriously, then something shifted. Up to that point, people made fun of me. People argued with me and pushed back about how much they needed their MQLs. The people who were profiting off the B2B Marketing World staying the same tried to discredit me.

But about 9 months in, in January 2020, credible people started reaching out to me privately - in the LinkedIn DMs and on phone calls.

All telling me I was right. They understood what I was sharing, and they were seeing the same thing. A few months later, growth accelerated rapidly for years. Unstoppable. And we positively impacted the lives of thousands of B2B marketers, hundreds of B2B companies, and changed the landscape of B2B marketing forever.

To me, it was always INEVITABLE. It just took a little time for the world to catch up.

Well, now we're 9 months into building ENCODED. And the same thing started happening 2 weeks ago. Phone calls, DMs, voice notes. Credible, experienced, talented people telling me they get it now.

And most importantly - the people who are actually using it. People calling ENCODED:

- "Life Changing"
- "Monumental"
- "Mind Blowing"

6 months ago, people were calling me a marketing grifter lol

Fast forward to now, more than 500 people have been positively impacted by using the ENCODED system. That's 500 lives. And that impact ripples into their relationships, families, teams, and communities.

Again, INEVITABLE. It just takes a little while for the world to catch up.

When you're challenging the status quo and paving new roads, the only question is can you deal with being misunderstood while the World catches up. Most people can't. They fold like a cheap chair. The resistance feels like rejection. The people trying to discredit you feel like proof you're wrong.

They care too much about what other people think of them, so they dismantle their vision to fit into what people will like, understand, and praise. And in that process, they lose all the magic of their vision.

People making fun of you early is actually a GOOD sign. It means you're threatening a status quo that people have grown comfortable with.

The real signal to pay attention to is different. It's the private DMs. The quiet phone calls. The people who get it but aren't ready to say it publicly yet. That's your leading indicator. That's the world catching up in real time.

When you KNOW it's inevitable, HOLD THE LINE. The world will catch up to your vision.

## Post 2

**URL:** [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_every-category-creating-company-gets-called-activity-7447039628118872066-EVS3](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_every-category-creating-company-gets-called-activity-7447039628118872066-EVS3) **Posted:** ~April 2026 **Reactions:** 159

Every category-creating company gets called a scam before it gets called a category.

Airbnb was dismissed as a liability nightmare by hotel chains. Who would let strangers sleep in their home? Now it's a $75 billion company that created the entire short-term rental category.

Uber was called illegal and unsafe by taxi companies. Nobody would get into a strangers car ordered from a phone app. It got banned in multiple cities before it was accepted. Now it's worth over $100 billion.

Salesforce was called technically naive and commercially unviable by SAP and Oracle. It invented the SaaS category and is now worth over $200 billion.

Peloton was called overpriced and gimmicky by traditional gyms and fitness equipment companies. It created the connected fitness category and hit a $50 billion valuation.

The critics were never the customers. The people loudest about why something won't work are almost always the people who've never tried it. And often the people with the most to lose if it succeeds.

Here's how I learned this firsthand. Twice.

First at Refine Labs. When I started saying the MQL model was broken. People laughed at me. Criticized me. They defended the MQL model they'd been using for the past 10+ years because that's what the board required. Tech vendors tried to discredit me because it was more profitable for them if the world stayed the same. Eventually, it became a common best practice in B2B Marketing.

Then I launched ENCODED. A training system targeting the subconscious mind that breaks the existing paradigm of how people think about personal and professional growth. And some people who had never met me and never tried the product had a lot to say:

- "Marketing grifter"
- "Spiritual coaching pyramid scheme"
- "Pseudoscience"

Meanwhile our actual customers were saying this:

- "My anxiety is already down 70% after 2 weeks."
- "I quit drinking after 2 months. It was easy."
- "My income doubled and my company runs without me. I didn't know this was possible."
- "Our relationship has gotten way stronger since we started doing ENCODED together."
- "Our family has so much more quality time now because I'm actually present."
- "This is the best investment I've made in my personal and professional life."
- "After 6 months the biggest change is basically no anxiety anymore."

The impact it has for people, their families, and their businesses is totally insane.

Market validation comes from the people who tried the product. Not trolls making uninformed comments on LinkedIn.

If you're building something new and important, the critics will always be there trying to tear you down. That's a signal you're onto something real.

Block out the noise. Stay focused on your customers. Stay true to your vision.
